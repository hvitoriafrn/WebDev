Amur Leopard's


Amur leopards live alone. Strong and with dexterous feet, it covers and moves incomplete kills to prevent other predators from stealing them.

Ecologically, commercially, and culturally, the Amur leopard is significant. Prey animals like deer and Amur tigers gain from the preservation of the habitat.

We can assure the region's long-term conservation by bringing them back with the appropriate conservation actions.

2 / 5

Black Rhino's


Lip shape is a distinguishing factor between black and white rhinos. White rhinos have square lips, but black rhinos have hooked upper lips.

In addition to having a significant impact on their ecosystems, rhinos are a major source of ecotourism revenue in several nations.

Elephants are among the many other species that benefit from black rhino conservation because these animals require vast lands to survive.

3 / 5

Sumatran Orangutan


The Sumatran orangutan inhabits tropical rainforests, where it lives surrounded by trees, almost exclusively.

Of the nine Sumatran orangutan populations that exist, only seven—each with an estimated 250 or more individuals—have any chance of surviving.

If orangutans were to disappear, so would several tree species, especially those with larger seeds.

4 / 5

Sumatran Tiger


The black stripes on their orange coats are what set Sunda tigers apart. On the island of Sumatra, the last of the Sunda island tigers fight to survive.

Increased deforestation and widespread poaching could lead to the extinction of this noble animal.

An essential sign of a forest's biodiversity and health is the existence of Sunda tigers.

5 / 5

Hawksbill


Hawksbills get their name from its small, sharp beak. They have a pattern of overlapping scales on their shells, giving the edges a serrated appearance.

Hawksbills are mostly found in coral reefs in the tropical waters of the planet.

They are a vital part of marine ecosystems, as Hawksbills contribute to coral reef health maintenance. They improve reef fish's access to food by clearing sponges and other prey from the reef's surface.
