Deforestation

Forests cover nearly one-third of the land area on our planet and are home to most of the worldâ€™s life on land.

They are also essential to human health, purifying our water and air and serving as our first line of defense against new infectious diseases.


Illegal Wildlife Trading

Wildlife crime is a big business. Run by dangerous international networks, wildlife and animal parts are trafficked much like illegal drugs and arms.

Corruption, toothless laws, weak judicial systems and light sentences allow criminal networks to keep plundering wildlife with little regard to consequences. These factors make illegal wildlife trade a low risk business with high returns.


Water Scarcity

Water covers 70% of our planet, and it is easy to think that it will always be plentiful

1.1 billion people worldwide lack access to water, and a total of 2.7 billion find water scarce for at least one month of the year.

Many of the water systems that keep ecosystems thriving and feed a growing human population have become stressed. Rivers, lakes and aquifers are drying up or becoming too polluted to use.
